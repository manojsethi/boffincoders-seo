# 07. Phase 4: Content Briefs And External AI Writing

## Phase Goal

Create content briefs from approved SEO recommendations and optionally use premium external AI models for writing.

Do not use small local models for final content writing.

## Why External AI For Writing

Content writing requires:

- better fluency.
- lower hallucination risk.
- better instruction following.
- better tone control.
- stronger reasoning for complex topics.

Use providers such as:

- OpenAI.
- Gemini.
- Anthropic.

Choose based on quality, cost, and task.

## Content Brief Inputs

- target keyword.
- secondary keywords.
- page role.
- page goal.
- current content summary.
- missing sections.
- competitor/SERP data if available.
- project goals.
- audience.
- brand tone.
- required internal links.
- schema recommendation.
- CTA.
- evidence.

## Content Brief Output

Brief should include:

- objective.
- target audience.
- search intent.
- title suggestions.
- meta description suggestions.
- H1.
- section outline.
- required talking points.
- FAQs.
- internal links.
- external citations needed if applicable.
- schema.
- CTA.
- what to avoid.
- validation checklist.

## Content Writing Workflow

1. Recommendation approved.
2. Generate brief.
3. Analyst edits brief.
4. Analyst selects external AI provider/model.
5. Generate draft.
6. Analyst reviews.
7. Content team edits.
8. Implement on website.
9. Re-crawl and re-audit.
10. Monitor GSC/GA4.

## Strict Safety Rule

For YMYL topics:

- healthcare.
- finance.
- legal.
- safety.
- education credentials.

AI content must require human expert review.

Do not publish AI draft directly.

## Provider Policy

Suggested:

| Task | Provider |
|---|---|
| Short title/meta options | local or external |
| Content brief | local draft + external refine |
| Full content draft | external only |
| Client-ready strategic wording | external preferred |
| YMYL content | external + human expert review |

## Validation Checklist

Do not mark complete until:

- brief generation uses evidence.
- external AI draft is optional, not automatic.
- analyst must review before publish.
- content draft links back to recommendation.
- implementation can be validated after crawl.
- self-audit is written.
